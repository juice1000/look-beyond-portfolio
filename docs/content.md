Selected case studies

Sprinkenhof

Turning fragmented document landscapes into decision-ready knowledge

Sprinkenhof manages a large and complex document environment with around 1.5 million files across many structures, classes, and permission layers. Important knowledge is distributed across documents, systems, and organizational contexts, which makes finding and using the right information difficult.

We are helping shape an AI-powered knowledge synthesis approach on top of this environment. The goal is not just retrieval, but meaningful access to internal knowledge in a way that supports faster, better-informed decisions. This includes working within enterprise constraints such as permissions, document structures, compliance requirements, and operational usability.

This case reflects a challenge many large organizations face: they already have the knowledge they need, but it is too difficult to surface, connect, and use effectively. Our role is to turn that hidden knowledge into decision support that works in practice.

What this enables
• faster access to relevant internal knowledge
• less time spent searching across complex document environments
• better-informed decision making in knowledge-heavy workflows
• a foundation for AI support in highly regulated enterprise contexts

⸻

FGS Global

Making enterprise AI safer through evaluation, monitoring, and operational control

At FGS Global, we have been deeply involved in strengthening the reliability of AI systems used in enterprise settings. The work focused on AIOps foundations for the Fergus ecosystem, with an emphasis on making AI behavior measurable, testable, and easier to improve over time.

This included designing and implementing evaluation approaches for AI capabilities, building structured testing logic, defining quality signals, and shaping analytics around how AI performs across development and operational stages. The broader goal has been clear: move beyond intuition and create an environment where AI systems can be trusted because they are continuously assessed.

This is critical for enterprises that want to scale AI responsibly. It is not enough for an assistant or agent to work sometimes. It needs to behave consistently, be monitored, and improve through feedback loops rather than guesswork.

What this enables
• more reliable AI performance in enterprise use cases
• earlier detection of quality issues and regressions
• clearer visibility into how AI systems behave over time
• safer rollout of AI across internal workflows and teams

⸻

Sourcera

Automating strategic workflow execution with AI

At Sourcera, the focus has been on AI workflows for procurement intelligence and supplier-related operations. The challenge was to reduce manual research and coordination work in areas where teams traditionally spend significant time gathering information, evaluating fit, and moving decisions forward.

We contributed to workflow-driven AI systems that support supplier discovery, capability tagging, retrieval-based intelligence, and structured processing of business-relevant information. Rather than offering a narrow chatbot experience, the value comes from building AI into the workflow itself, so that decisions and actions move faster with less manual effort.

This is a strong example of what operational AI should look like in enterprise settings: not a disconnected feature, but a system that reduces friction across real business processes.

What this enables
• less manual effort in research-heavy workflows
• faster progression from information gathering to decision support
• more scalable handling of repetitive knowledge tasks
• improved process efficiency in operational and procurement contexts
