# Look Beyond Solutions — Website Strategy Brief

**Purpose:** Strategic overview for repositioning the lookbeyond.sg website. To be used as a brief for Claude Code to optimize site content, structure, and messaging.

---

## 1. Current State Assessment

### What the site does well

The current site has a clear, professional identity built around "Enterprise AI. Engineered to operate." It effectively communicates three technical pillars (AI workflow systems, autonomous agents, AI performance monitoring), targets three industries (procurement, manufacturing, logistics), and has a logical engagement ladder (free call → workshop → POC → retainer).

### Core positioning gaps

**The site undersells what the company actually does.**

The case study portfolio reveals capabilities well beyond AI agents and workflow automation: full custom platform builds (replacing SaaS tools), ERP implementation and migration, cloud data infrastructure (BigQuery, GCP, dbt), MLOps pipelines, and Microsoft ecosystem integration. None of these appear as advertised services. A prospect visiting the site today would not know they could hire Look Beyond to replace their ERP or build them a custom platform from scratch.

**The biggest unaddressed opportunity is Custom Platform Development.**

An internal strategic discussion identified a significant market shift: as AI-assisted development drops the cost of building bespoke software, custom platforms are becoming competitive with off-the-shelf SaaS — without requiring businesses to adapt their processes to the software. Look Beyond has already executed this (NOA, Green Data), but it is invisible on the website. This is a first-mover positioning opportunity that is currently being left on the table.

**The site conflates strategic entry points with service delivery.**

"AI Readiness Assessment" and the workshop offerings are positioned as low-stakes sampling mechanisms. They are underutilized as strategic entry points that lead to longer-term engagements. The engagement model (data layer → platform → AI integration) is not communicated.

---

## 2. Proposed Positioning

### Core message (unchanged but sharpened)

**"Enterprise AI. Engineered to operate."** remains strong. The sharpening is in the supporting narrative:

> We build the systems enterprises actually need — custom platforms, AI workflow automation, and data infrastructure — designed around your processes, not the other way around.

### The strategic frame to introduce

The company's point of view is that the right AI transformation starts at the **data and process layer**, not at the employee surface (e.g., rolling out ChatGPT). This is a genuine differentiator and a strong hook for enterprise decision-makers. It should be surfaced more prominently — ideally in the hero or "What we solve" section — as a perspective, not just a service description.

---

## 3. Service Offering Architecture

The site should reflect two distinct but related service tracks:

### Track A — AI Systems (current, retain and refine)

Autonomous agents, AI workflow pipelines, performance monitoring. Already well-positioned. Continue with industry specialization in procurement, manufacturing, logistics.

- AI Workflow Systems
- Autonomous Agent Development
- AI Performance Monitoring & Governance
- Knowledge Retrieval Systems

### Track B — Custom Platform & Infrastructure (new, needs addition)

This track is documented in the case studies but not surfaced as a service offering. It should be explicit.

- **Custom Platform Development** — Full bespoke platforms built around the client's existing processes, priced and contracted on a subscription or retainer model. Direct alternative to off-the-shelf SaaS where the client no longer has to adapt to the software.
- **ERP Integration & Migration** — Connecting custom platforms or new ERP systems (e.g., ERPNext, Microsoft Navision/Business Central) to replace fragmented legacy tooling.
- **Cloud Data Infrastructure** — Data warehouse design and build (BigQuery, GCP, dbt), MLOps pipelines, CI/CD workflows. Referenced in Expresssteuer, SAMGEN, R-Cycle case studies but absent from the services section.

### Track C — Strategy & Enablement (current, needs elevation)

Currently exists but is framed too narrowly as workshop formats.

- **AI-Native Transformation Strategy** — Strategic advisory starting from the data and process layer. The argument: AI transformation that starts with end-user tooling (ChatGPT rollouts) without fixing the underlying data and process foundation delivers no durable value. Look Beyond helps companies sequence this correctly.
- **Workshops** (retain the three formats — Knowledge Synthesis, Workflow Automation, Reliability & Governance)
- **AI Readiness Assessment** (retain)

---

## 4. Messaging Hierarchy

### Hero / Above the fold

Lead with the sharpest version of the core message. Avoid leading with tool names or agent types — lead with the outcome and the point of view.

Suggested direction:

> "Software that fits your business. AI that runs in production."

Or keep the existing headline and sharpen the subheadline to include the platform angle alongside the AI workflow angle.

### Problem framing ("What we solve")

Currently focused on AI-specific friction (fragmented tools, AI pilots that stall). Broaden to include:

- The cost and disruption of adapting to off-the-shelf SaaS
- The risk of starting AI transformation at the wrong layer (employees vs. data/processes)

### Solution framing ("What we build")

Add Custom Platform Development and Cloud Infrastructure as explicit solution categories alongside the AI pillars. Each should have a one-line value proposition:

- Custom platforms → "Built for your processes. Priced like SaaS."
- ERP & integration → "Connect or replace legacy systems without disrupting operations."
- Infrastructure → "Data foundations that make AI work in production."

### Industries

Retain procurement, manufacturing, logistics. Consider adding a fourth: **Professional Services / Advisory** — given the FGS Global case study (M&A, IPO advisory AI platform), this is a validated vertical worth naming.

### Case Studies

Several strong case studies (Strict, Expresssteuer, SAMGEN, R-Cycle, Cinoware, SAMGEN) are in the portfolio but either not prominent or not tied to a named service. Each should be linked to a service track so prospects can self-identify.

---

## 5. Engagement Model — Clarify the Path

The current engagement ladder (free call → $2K workshop → $15K POC → retainer) is solid but doesn't reflect the custom platform track, which has a different sales motion. Add a parallel path:

**For AI workflow / agent work:** Free call → Discovery Workshop → POC → Retainer  
**For custom platform builds:** Free call → Scoping session → Fixed-scope build → Subscription/maintenance retainer

The distinction matters because the custom platform pitch is closer to "replace your Navision subscription with something built for you" — that's a different buyer conversation than "automate your procurement intake."

---

## 6. Page-Level Recommendations

### Home page

- Broaden the hero subheadline to reference both AI systems and custom platform development
- Add a "point of view" section (even brief) that articulates why transformation should start at the data/process layer
- Add Custom Platform Development as a fourth pillar alongside the three AI pillars, or restructure into two tracks (AI Systems / Custom Platforms)
- Surface 1–2 additional industry examples (e.g., Professional Services via FGS, Tax/Finance via Expresssteuer)

### Services / Solutions page (or new section)

- Explicitly list Track B services (Custom Platform Development, ERP Integration, Cloud Infrastructure) — these currently only appear buried in case study copy
- Frame each service around the business problem it solves, not just the technology

### Case Studies page

- Tag each case study by service track so visitors can filter by what they need
- Elevate Strict (ERP + AI transformation) and SAMGEN (infrastructure + MLOps) — these are strong proof points for Track B that currently get less visibility than the AI-agent-focused studies

### Pricing & Engagement page

- Add a second engagement path for custom platform builds
- Consider renaming "POC development" to "Pilot build" — more accessible language for non-technical buyers

### Workshops page

- Position workshops more explicitly as a strategic entry point, not just standalone products
- Add a line connecting each workshop to the longer-term engagement it typically leads to

---

## 7. What Not to Change

- The "Enterprise AI. Engineered to operate." headline — strong, keep it
- The three-pillar AI system structure — well-executed, just needs a fourth pillar or a parallel track
- The engagement ladder pricing — transparent and credible
- The industry depth (procurement, manufacturing, logistics) — differentiated and supported by real work
- The security / reliability controls section — this is a real differentiator for enterprise buyers and should stay prominent

---

## 8. Priority Order

1. Add Custom Platform Development as an explicit service offering (highest impact, currently zero presence)
2. Broaden the "What we solve" and "What we build" sections to reflect the full service scope
3. Add the AI-native transformation point of view to the homepage
4. Tag and resurface case studies by service track
5. Add a second engagement path for platform builds on the Pricing page
6. Consider adding Professional Services as a fourth named industry vertical
